Recovering Hands and Objects from Internet Videos
Anonymous CVPR24 Submission
Paper ID 1527

Abstract

We study the problem of recovering hands and objects from Internet videos. This requires understanding the hand-object interactions in 4D, spatially in 3D and over time, which is challenging due to mutual hand-object occlusions. In this paper we present a novel reconstruction technique RHOV (Recovering Hands and Objects from Videos), which reconstructs 4D trajectories of both the hands and the objects. We leverage large pre-trained models for identifying and segmenting the objects that the hands are interacting with and formulate an optimization problem to recover the 4D hand-object trajectories per video, consistent in 3D and over time. We apply our reconstruction technique to over 100 challenging Internet videos and evaluate it on in-the-lab datasets as well. Finally, we show that our recovered trajectories can be used in downstream applications by imitating the recovered object interactions on a robot arm in a physics simulator with reinforcement learning.

Reconstructions from Internet Videos

To reconstruct hands and objects from Internet videos, we introduce an optimization-based technique, called RHOV (for Reconstructing Hands and Objects from Videos), leveraging 2D spatial cues (keypoints, masks, depth) and temporal smoothness constraints (4D, optical flow). Ablation Studies

RHOV jointly reconstructs the hand and object across all frames of the video at once. We conduct a series of ablation studies to further understand the importance of joint reconstruction. In particular, we find that a) reconstructing hands and objects independently produces a reconstruction where the hand is far from the object and b) reconstructing hands and objects for each video frame separately leads to flipping in the object model due to symmetrical ambiguities.

Imitating Object Interactions

To show an application of the RHOV reconstructions in downstream tasks, we present an approach for imitating the object interactions from the video reconstructions. We first reconstruct hand-object trajectories in 4D, 3D plus time. We then train a policy to imitate the reconstructed object trajectory with reinforcement learning on a robot arm in a physics simulator.
